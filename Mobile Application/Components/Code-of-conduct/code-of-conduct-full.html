1. Introduction

Kombz Leap Enterprises Ltd is committed to maintaining the highest standards of ethical conduct, professionalism, and integrity. This Code of Conduct serves as a guide for all employees, regardless of position or location, to understand their responsibilities and the standards expected of them.

Scope

This Code applies to all employees, contractors and anyone acting on behalf of Kombz Leap Enterprises Ltd. Compliance is mandatory, and violations may result in disciplinary action up to and including termination.

2. Core Values & Ethics

Our company is built on a foundation of core values that guide every decision and action:

  • IntegrityWe act honestly, transparently, and ethically in all business dealings.
  • Excellence: We strive for quality and continuous improvement.
  • Accountability: We take responsibility for our actions and decisions.
  • Teamwork: We collaborate respectfully to achieve common goals.
  • Respect for Human Rights: We uphold the dignity and rights of all individuals.

⚖️ Zero Tolerance for Corruption

Kombz Leap Enterprises maintains a strict zero-tolerance policy against fraud, bribery, and corruption. Employees must never offer, give, or accept bribes, kickbacks, or improper benefits.

3. Workplace Conduct

Professional Behavior

  • Treat all colleagues, customers, and stakeholders with respect and courtesy.
  • Follow all lawful instructions from supervisors and management.
  • Maintain punctuality and adhere to working hours as per the Employment Manual.
  • Refrain from using profane, offensive, or discriminatory language.

Attendance & Punctuality

  • Employees must record attendance daily.
  • Unexcused absenteeism or tardiness is subject to disciplinary action.
  • All absences must be reported in advance to the immediate supervisor.

4. Harassment & Discrimination Policy

Kombz Leap Enterprises is committed to providing a workplace free from harassment, discrimination, and gender-based violence.

  • Sexual Harassment: Unwelcome advances, requests for favors, or other conduct of a sexual nature is strictly prohibited.
  • Discrimination: No employee shall be discriminated against based on gender, race, religion, age, disability, marital status, or any other protected characteristic.
  • Gender-Based-Violence (GBV)The company maintains a zero-tolerance approach and will take immediate action against any form of violence or abuse.

📢 Reporting Harassment

If you experience or witness harassment, report it immediately to your supervisor, HR, or use the confidential reporting channels. All reports will be investigated promptly and discreetly.

5. Use of Company resources and properties

  • Company property, including vehicles, equipment, and computers, must be used for business purposes only.
  • Employees are responsible for any loss or damage due to negligence or misuse.
  • Unauthorized removal or use of company property is considered misconduct and may lead to disciplinary action.
  • Personal use of company resources must be minimal and approved by management.

6. Confidentiality & Data Protection

  • All employee, customer, and business information must be treated as strictly confidential.
  • Confidential information must not be shared with unauthorized persons, even after employment ends.
  • Employees must comply with data protection principles and safeguard personal data.
  • Breach of confidentiality is a serious offense and may result in immediate dismissal.

7. Disciplinary Process

Disciplinary actions are designed to correct behavior and ensure compliance with company standards. The process includes progressive steps:

  1. Verbal WarningIssued for minor infractions.
  2. Written WarningFor repeated or more serious violations.
  3. Final WarningFinal notice before termination.
  4. TerminationFor gross misconduct or failure to improve after prior warnings.

8. Reporting Violations

If you suspect a violation of this Code of Conduct, you have a responsibility to report it. Retaliation against anyone who reports in good faith is strictly prohibited.

  • Report to your immediate supervisor or department head.
  • If uncomfortable, report directly to Human Resources.
  • Anonymous reports may be submitted via the confidential reporting system.
